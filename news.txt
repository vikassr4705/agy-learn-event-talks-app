# Latest World News Headlines - June 16, 2026

## West Asia: U.S.–Iran Peace Deal
* **De-escalation Efforts:** Following weeks of tense negotiations, the U.S. and Iran have reached an agreement to end hostilities. President Donald Trump announced that ships are beginning to move through the Strait of Hormuz again, which will be "completely open" by June 19, 2026.
* **Deal Details:** The agreement is outlined in a brief memorandum of understanding (MoU). While President Trump stated that Iran has agreed to never pursue a nuclear weapon, officials noted that the document is general and defers some of the most complex issues. Pakistan’s Prime Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif confirmed that Islamabad will host the formal signing ceremony in Geneva on Friday.

## Major International Incidents & Policy Changes
* **B-52 Bomber Crash:** Eight people are believed to have died after a U.S. Air Force B-52 Stratofortress crashed during a routine test flight in California. Military officials stated there was no chance of survivors.
* **UK Social Media Ban:** British Prime Minister Keir Starmer announced a new policy banning under-16s from accessing all major social media apps, citing it as the "right choice" for the government.
* **EU Sanctions on Russia:** The European Union has adopted a fresh package of sanctions targeting Russia, specifically focusing on the country's "shadow fleet," military-industrial complex, and energy revenues.
